How is Reputation Useful

  • From Perspective of Observers:
    • Aids decisions amidst uncertainty
    • Useful when information is scarce
    • Fills in for the unknown

Puzzle

  • Assume that prior outcomes are perfectly observable?
  • What happens when they are not?
  • Example: A holder enters into an alliance, but no members ever encounter conflict.
    • What does this tell observers about the holder's type?

Theory

  • Threshold:
  • \(P^{*}=\frac{U\_{os}(Reject)-U\_{os}(Betrayl)}{U\_{os}(Accept|Reliable)-U\_{os}(Betrayl)}\)
  • Conditions for Acceptance:
  • Pr(Reliable)\(\geq P^{*}\)

Comparing ambiguous outcomes against Traditional Model

Hypotheses:

  • H1: Holder reputations with observers decay faster among recently betrayed observers.
  • H2: Holders that enter into agreements with ambiguous outcomes have better reputations than holders that did not enter into any such agreements
  • H3: Among holders that recently entered into agreements with ambiguous outcomes, holder reputations with recently betrayed observers will decay faster than holder reputations with observers that have not recently been betrayed.
